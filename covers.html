Covers

Songs Jimi Hendrix covered and artists who covered Jimi Hendrix

Songs Jimi Hendrix Covered

Jimi Hendrix's interpretations of songs by other artists, often becoming definitive versions.

All Along the Watchtower

Originally by Bob Dylan (1967)

Covered in 1968 from Electric Ladyland

Hendrix's interpretation became so definitive that Dylan himself adopted the arrangement for his own live performances. Dylan said, 'It overwhelmed me, really. He had such talent.'

Considered the greatest cover version ever recorded

Like a Rolling Stone

Originally by Bob Dylan (1965)

Covered in 1967 from Live at Monterey

Performed at Monterey Pop Festival, paying tribute to Dylan while making it his own with extended guitar work.

Monterey Pop Festival performance

Hey Joe

Originally by Billy Roberts (1962)

Covered in 1966 from Are You Experienced

While written by Billy Roberts, Hendrix's slow, blues-inflected version became the definitive recording and his breakthrough single.

First UK single, reached #6

Wild Thing

Originally by The Troggs (1966)

Covered in 1967 from Live at Monterey

The explosive finale to his Monterey Pop performance, ending with the infamous guitar burning.

Guitar burning performance at Monterey

Johnny B. Goode

Originally by Chuck Berry (1958)

Covered in 1970 from Live at Berkeley

Hendrix pays tribute to one of his guitar heroes with an electrifying live version.

Tribute to rock pioneer Chuck Berry

Sgt. Pepper's Lonely Hearts Club Band

Originally by The Beatles (1967)

Covered in 1967 from Live performance

Just days after the album's release, Hendrix opened his London show with this cover, with George Harrison and Paul McCartney in attendance.

Performed 3 days after album release with Beatles present

Killing Floor

Originally by Howlin' Wolf (1964)

Covered in 1967 from Live at Monterey

A tribute to blues master Howlin' Wolf, performed at Monterey Pop Festival.

Blues tribute at Monterey

Rock Me Baby

Originally by B.B. King (1964)

Covered in 1967 from Live at Monterey

Paying homage to B.B. King at Monterey Pop Festival.

B.B. King tribute

Sunshine of Your Love

Originally by Cream (1967)

Covered in 1969 from Live (BBC)

A tribute to Eric Clapton and Cream, famously played on the Lulu Show when Hendrix stopped playing Hey Joe mid-song to honor Cream's breakup.

Spontaneous tribute on BBC television
